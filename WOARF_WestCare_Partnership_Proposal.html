Jason — WestCare has been uplifting the human spirit in Nevada for decades, serving the populations most impacted by OUD: people experiencing homelessness, justice-involved individuals, women and children, veterans, and rural communities without adequate access to care. ECQO-Care is not asking WestCare to do something new. It is asking WestCare to be the human endpoint of a system that ensures the people who need WestCare most can actually find their way there — and stay connected once they do. This proposal describes exactly how that works, and what the partnership means for the county.

What the 2026–2029 Needs Assessment Found

Washoe County Has Services.
The Gap Is Getting People to Them.

The Needs Assessment found that survey respondents felt services were accessible — but expressed deep concern about the county's ability to coordinate and navigate people to those services. That navigation gap is what ECQO-Care fills, with WestCare as the destination.

FINDING 01 / NAVIGATION

Outreach Worker Funding Ended October 2025

The community outreach workers who successfully connected disconnected populations to care lost their funding in October 2025. ECQO-Care deploys always-available digital outreach that operates where those workers left off — routing individuals to WestCare and clinical partners through structured warm handoffs the moment elevated risk is detected.

FINDING 02 / ACCESS

The Window of Readiness Is Narrow and Unforgiving

The Needs Assessment identifies the "short window of readiness for change" as the most critical — and most commonly missed — intervention point. Long waitlists, insurance denials, and lack of real-time navigation cause individuals to miss that window. ECQO catches them the moment they engage and connects them directly to WestCare before the window closes.

FINDING 03 / STIGMA

Stigma and Discrimination Deter Help-Seeking

The Needs Assessment documents that stigma from healthcare providers and community members actively deters individuals from seeking help. ECQO's Virtual Human effect — proven to increase disclosure rates for stigmatized behaviors — creates the stigma-free first contact that makes WestCare's door feel reachable for people who would otherwise never knock.

FINDING 04 / WORKFORCE

Provider Burnout Is Eroding the System From Within

The Needs Assessment documents the Service Provider Burnout Cycle in detail: high-acuity caseloads, secondary trauma, and low wages drive providers out of the field faster than they can be replaced. ECQO absorbs 90% of Tier 1 prevention volume, protecting WestCare's clinical capacity for the complex, high-acuity work that only trained humans can do.

WestCare Nevada, Inc.'s Four Roles

The Human Infrastructure
Behind Every ECQO Handoff.

WestCare does not receive referrals from ECQO-Care. WestCare is ECQO-Care's most critical partner — the verified, trusted, staffed human endpoint that every warm handoff is designed to reach.

🔄

Warm-Handoff Receiving Partner

When ECQO's AI Moderators detect elevated OUD risk signals, they activate a warm-handoff protocol. WestCare Nevada, Inc. is the primary community receiving partner — verified, MOU-documented, and staffed to receive those referrals and complete the connection. WestCare's no-insurance, no-ability-to-pay policy makes it the right destination for every population ECQO-Care serves. Referral completion rates are tracked and reported to WOARF quarterly.

🌍

Community Outreach + Participant Access

WestCare's deep community penetration across Washoe County — through its outreach infrastructure, Nevada State Welfare Outreach Enroller onsite, and relationships with justice-involved, homeless, veteran, and rural populations — provides ECQO-Care with trusted access to the individuals the Needs Assessment identifies as hardest to reach. WestCare channels support participant recruitment and ensure outreach reaches those most at risk.

👥

Peer Workforce Pipeline for Certification Cohorts

WestCare's direct service staff and peer recovery community include individuals with lived experience of OUD and behavioral addiction — exactly the population ECQO-Care's ARM, AM, and ANS certification tracks are designed for. WestCare serves as a recruitment pipeline for peer navigators entering ECQO certification cohorts, extending career development opportunities to the workforce WestCare already serves and employs.

📋

Referral Directory Anchor + Compliance Linkage

WestCare's program breadth — residential treatment, outpatient, transitional housing, veteran services, women and children's programming — means it anchors ECQO's live partner referral directory across multiple population-specific pathways. ECQO AI Navigator Specialists maintain current WestCare referral protocols, eligibility criteria, and intake procedures, ensuring warm handoffs arrive with the context WestCare needs to connect participants quickly.

Population Alignment

Every WestCare Program Serves
a Population ECQO-Care Targets.

The 2026–2029 Needs Assessment identifies eight populations experiencing disproportionate OUD impact. WestCare Nevada, Inc. serves six of them directly — making this partnership the most population-complete referral relationship in the program.

POPULATION 01

People Experiencing Homelessness

WestCare's 4th Street Transitional Living, Women and Children's Campus, and Homefront VA programs serve individuals without stable housing. ECQO's mobile-first, no-address-required platform reaches them where they are — and routes them to WestCare with context and a warm introduction already established.

POPULATION 02

Women + Gender-Diverse Individuals

WestCare's Women and Children's Campus and Women Warrior Unit provide specialized residential, transitional, and veteran services for women — including the Serenity Program and Healthy Families Project. ECQO's warm handoff protocols include gender-responsive referral pathways that route directly to these programs.

POPULATION 03

Veterans

WestCare's Homefront VA Independent Housing program in Reno directly serves veterans experiencing OUD and housing instability. ECQO-Care's referral directory includes veteran-specific escalation pathways that route to WestCare Reno for this population — one of the Needs Assessment's explicitly prioritized groups.

POPULATION 04

Justice-Involved Individuals

WestCare's Criminal Justice programming provides treatment and recovery support for individuals involved in the justice system. The Needs Assessment documents that 130 people per month have OUD treatment interrupted by jail contact. ECQO's reentry warm-handoff protocols connect justice-involved individuals back to WestCare upon community return.

POPULATION 05

Uninsured + Medicaid Recipients

WestCare's onsite Nevada State Welfare Outreach Enroller and no-turn-away policy eliminates the insurance barrier the Needs Assessment documents as a primary access obstacle. ECQO's warm handoffs explicitly note WestCare's no-insurance policy to reduce participant hesitation at the point of referral.

POPULATION 06

Youth + Young Adults (18–35)

WestCare's Education and Prevention programming, including Connections Fatherhood F.I.R.E., reaches young adults and families. ECQO's co-designed, mobile-first platform meets this demographic in their digital environment and routes to WestCare's prevention and early intervention programs before escalation occurs.

How ECQO-Care + WestCare
Answer Priority 3 Together.

No single organization can answer all dimensions of Priority 3. ECQO-Care and WestCare together cover every requirement — digital infrastructure, human warm handoff, cultural outreach, workforce development, and sustainability.

3

Multidisciplinary OUD Prevention + Education

ECQO delivers clinically governed AI prevention — existential risk screening, OUD psychoeducation, cognitive distortion interruption, and coping skill micro-interventions — 24/7 to adults at risk. WestCare provides the residential, outpatient, and transitional services that ECQO participants are navigated to when clinical support is needed. Together they form a complete prevention-to-treatment continuum.

↳

Culturally Appropriate Outreach for Under-Resourced Communities

WestCare's no-turn-away policy, onsite benefits enroller, and statewide program presence ensure that ECQO's warm handoffs reach populations regardless of insurance, documentation, or housing status. WestCare's community relationships with justice-involved, veteran, and women-serving programs extend ECQO's cultural reach beyond what a technology platform can achieve alone.

↳

Workforce Sustainability and Burnout Mitigation

WestCare's clinical and peer staff benefit directly from ECQO absorbing Tier 1 prevention volume — routine check-ins, psychoeducation, emotional tracking — that currently occupies frontline worker capacity. WestCare staff are also eligible to enter ECQO's ARM, AM, and ANS certification tracks, creating new professional development pathways and supplemental income opportunities that directly address the retention crisis the Needs Assessment documents.

↳

Sustainability: Navigation Over Expansion

The Needs Assessment's most cited finding is that coordination and navigation — not service expansion — is the primary unmet need. ECQO-Care does not add competing services to WestCare's landscape. It adds the navigation infrastructure that fills the gap between a person's moment of readiness and WestCare's door. This partnership is additive, not duplicative. Desert Rose Gives is not a Medicaid provider; WestCare is — and the partnership structure honors that distinction clearly.

Implementation Roadmap

How the Partnership
Activates and Scales.

P1
Phase 1 · April – June 2026 · Formalization

MOU Execution + Referral Infrastructure Setup

Execute Memorandum of Understanding between Desert Rose Gives, ECQO Holdings, and WestCare Nevada, Inc. defining warm-handoff protocols, referral directory documentation, data sharing governance, and staff contacts for escalation response. WestCare program leads are interviewed for ECQO referral directory: intake criteria, capacity, population specialties, response time commitments, and escalation contacts confirmed for all relevant program pathways.

P2
Phase 2 · July – December 2026 · Pilot Launch

Warm Handoffs Live + Workforce Cohort Launch

ECQO platform deploys with 100+ participants. First warm handoffs to WestCare initiated and tracked. WestCare peer recovery staff recruited for ARM certification cohort (up to 5 positions available through WestCare pipeline). Monthly partner learning sessions begin — WestCare designated staff attend to review de-identified referral completion data and provide feedback on intake friction, resource gaps, and protocol refinements.

P3
Phase 3 · 2027 · Scale + Evidence Build

Expanded Referral Network + Certification Expansion

Year 1 warm-handoff completion data and referral follow-through metrics inform protocol refinements. WestCare-certified ARM graduates eligible for AM cohort enrollment. Quarterly WOARF reports include WestCare-confirmed linkage outcomes. Partner data contributes to BeHERE NV's independent evaluation and the joint SAMHSA/HRSA federal grant co-application.

Grant Objectives
The WOARF grant funds Desert Rose Gives to deploy the ECQO conversational AI platform as 24/7 behavioral health prevention infrastructure for Washoe County adults — addressing OUD and co-occurring behavioral addictions through a multidisciplinary, peer-governed, tech-enabled model. ECQO delivers existential risk screening, OUD psychoeducation, cognitive distortion interruption, and warm handoffs to clinical partners. Over three years, the program trains 20 peer navigators in AI-integrated behavioral health roles (ECQO-ARM, ECQO-AM, ECQO-ANS) and builds an evidence base for NSHE curriculum integration and federal sustainability funding.
ECQO-Care Program Goals
Goal 1 — Prevention Infrastructure: Deploy ECQO as always-available, stigma-free digital prevention and navigation infrastructure ensuring no individual misses their window of readiness due to waitlists, transportation, insurance barriers, or shame.

Goal 2 — Workforce Development: Build Washoe County's first certified AI-specialized peer behavioral health workforce through ECQO-ARM, ECQO-AM, and ECQO-ANS certifications — creating career advancement pathways for peers and CHWs trapped in entry-level roles.

Goal 3 — Warm Handoff System: Operationalize a verified, partner-confirmed warm handoff network with WestCare Nevada, Inc. as the primary receiving partner — ensuring every elevated-risk escalation reaches a staffed, documented human endpoint within 72 hours.
